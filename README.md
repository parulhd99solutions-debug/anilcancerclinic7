Early Warning Symptoms of Breast Cancer
1. Lump or Thickening of the Breast and Armpit




Among the most prevalent early warning symptoms is that there will be a lump or a thickening of the breast and armpit. However, not all lumps will be cancerous.




2. Changes in the Size or Shape of the Breast




Any sudden or unexpected change in the size or shape of a breast should warrant a visit to a doctor's office.




3. Changes in the Breast Skin




Be alert to changes in the colour or texture of the breast skin. It is not uncommon for the skin to become reddish or pitted.




4. Any Change in Nipples




Changes in nipples should alert someone that there might be something wrong. Inversion, pain, or the presence of blood-tinged discharge are some indications.




5. Persistent or Unusual Breast Pain




Persistent pain or discomfort in the breast should raise concerns. Although rare, breast cancer can occur without a palpable lump.




6. Breast Swelling




Swelling of the breast tissue without the presence of a palpable lump can be another indication.




7. Weight Loss or Extreme Fatigue




Some people can experience unexplained weight loss or exhaustion when dealing with early-stage breast cancer.





Significance of Regular Screenings for Breast Cancer Treatment 




Mammograms and clinical breast examinations help in identifying cancer early, when there are no symptoms. Women aged over 40 years or those who have a history of breast cancer should be screened regularly. Diagnosis allows patients to avail themselves of breast cancer treatment.

2. Is breast discomfort a sign of cancer?




Breast tenderness does not always indicate that someone has breast cancer. The situation requires medical attention when the pain persists. Immediate evaluation leads to accurate diagnosis and results in patient reassurance.





3. How frequently should women be screened?




Women who are 40 years old or older should receive annual mammogram screenings. Individuals at high risk need to start their screening tests at an earlier time. You can determine your required screening frequency by consulting your doctor.
